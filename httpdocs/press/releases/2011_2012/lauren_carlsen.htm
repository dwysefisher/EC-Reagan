Posted - November 15, 2011

 

Eureka College Sophomore Uncovers New Reagan Information for Book ‘Reagan’s Roots’

 

A Eureka College sophomore’s hours of work in the college Archives has resulted in new information about Ronald Reagan’s college days that will be included in a new book about Reagan’s Illinois years.  

Lauren Carlsen of Bellingham, Wash., is the student assistant at the Ronald Reagan Museum and the student assistant researcher at the college Archives. She discovered information that surprised college officials and will be included in Peter Hannaford’s book “Reagan’s Roots” that will be unveiled in a book tour in Central Illinois in early December.

Carlsen found much of the information as she examined every copy of the Pegasus student newspaper from 1928 to1932, the years Reagan attended the school.

“It was surprising to read that when Reagan and Margaret Cleaver became engaged, Reagan bought 5 pounds of Fannie May chocolates for Margaret's sorority sisters,” Carlsen said. “Reagan was by no means a rich man, so for him to buy that much chocolate was very interesting,” she said.

Also of note was that midway through the first semester of Reagan’s junior year he became president of the Booster Club when the senior who held the elected position suddenly resigned and appointed Reagan. 

“The Booster Club president's duties were to plan three very full days of homecoming activities,” Carlsen said, “including judging events like the pajama race, house decorating and more. It must have been very time-consuming, especially since he already was a member of the football team.”

Carlsen said she feels she’s gotten to know Reagan the student. “Reagan was a people person and had a passion for being around others” she said. “He was so involved in campus life that it seems there rarely was a moment when he was lazy. I think his campus involvement and the way he treated others are what made him someone that all students should model.”            

Besides student newspapers, Carlsen read yearbooks and first-hand accounts written by Reagan classmates. She identified more than 100 dates that reveal what Reagan was doing, who he was with and where he was. College catalogs helped to determine his final exams schedules. Some of the information will appear in Hannaford’s book as an appendix titled "Reagan Chronology at Eureka College.” It will include his course schedules, which are being published for the first time, Carlsen said.

Carlsen spends about 10 hours a week in her student assistant role, which includes leading tours of the Reagan Museum.

“I really have a thirst for knowledge when it comes to the history of Eureka College, and Reagan is a part of that history,” she said. “With my new-found research, I am able to provide better tours to groups that visit the museum, and I have a better appreciation for how Reagan’s years at Eureka College influenced the person and leader he became.”

Carlsen is a daughter of Lance Carlsen of Bellingham and Kristin Carlsen of Bellevue, Wash. She is a 2010 graduate of Squalicum High School, Bellingham. She is majoring in music with teacher certification.
