Meet Natalie - A Junior Reagan Fellow

 

"Eureka College has made such a difference in my life and
I know that I would not be as successful at any other university as I am here due to the wonderful rollercoaster ride I have been placed on since my arrival."

 

Why did you choose your school?

Being the third child in to enter into college from my family I had already heard and knew a lot about college from my two older sisters. My older sister, Alyssa, is just a year ahead of me in school and so when she decided to attend Eureka College was the first time I had ever really heard of Eureka. Therefore, when I started really looking and considering colleges my senior year and I realized that I wanted to go to a smaller school versus a large university my mom told me to give Eureka College a consideration by applying for a the Reagan Fellowship Program so I could stay on campus for the weekend and see if I would enjoy the small campus life. I went to the weekend competition and fell in love with how welcoming the professors, staff, and students were. Therefore, when I received a phone call a few weeks later saying that I had been selected for the Reagan Fellowship Program I gladly accepted.


How would you describe your college experience so far?

Plan and simple a rollercoaster! Eureka has provided me with so many new ups, downs, turns, upside downs, screams, laughs, and of course thrill! Since my journey in college has begun I have been pushed to my limits through activities, classes, and opportunities. Eureka has given me the chance to really become an individual. It seems that everytime I talk with one of my mentors at school they have some new information that just makes my life so much more exciting. Since entering college I have been the chance to travel to Europe, Costa Rica, Washington D.C., North Carolina, and am getting ready to leave for a semester in Newcastle, Australia. The rollercoaster isn't just defined though by the amount of traveling I have done so far, but really has came from the great memories of being part of the Residential Life staff, dorm experiences, classroom assignments, and everyday life. I can't wait to see what else the Eureka rollercoaster as in store for me.


Do you participate in any activities or organizations?

Thus far at Eureka, I am involved in Leadership Ambassadors, Freshman Seminar Peer Instructor, Up Till Dawn Executive Board, Residential Life, The Maroon and Golden Rule, Swim Team, and Tennis Team. I was always involved in high school. In college I took involvement to a new level though. I don't regret being in so much because it has really helped me develop as a person and has given me experiences I would not have gotten any other way.


How have you changed or grown since starting college?

Besides looking a little older in the face nothing on the outside had changed about me since my college adventure has started. The changes that have taken place on the inside of me though are beyond comprehendible. The amount of personal growth in maturity, knowledge, inspiration to make change, sense of adventure, independence, and so much more have been altered for the better. For this I am ever thankful for the opportunities Eureka College has opened up for me.
