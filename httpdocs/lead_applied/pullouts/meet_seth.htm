Meet Seth - A Junior Reagan Fellow

 

"Isn't it interesting how one decision can change your entire life?
Eureka College was that kind of decision for me."

 

My life-changing decision began when I entered into an International Contest when I was an 8th grader living in Ghana, West Africa. This contest was a foreign exchange program sponsored by NASA and Dr. Mae Jemison, the first African-American woman to travel in space. Through The Earth We Share Foundation, I was selected to represent our country and moved to the states to attend high school and college, which eventually led me to Eureka College.

 

I love Eureka College. It's a small school where students can do big things. It's a place where professors know my name and my story. I'm so lucky these opportunities were offered to me through the high school exchange program and through the Ronald W. Reagan Leadership Program. I didn't apply for these scholarships for the recognition. I applied because I wanted to show people they too could come from nowhere and end up somewhere.

 

I haven't seen my family in four years and that's been the hardest part for me. The people at Eureka College have become my family. I've met so many people with diverse backgrounds, all working hand-in-hand, striving toward the Eureka idea. Even after one semester, I could see my classmates and I were more grown up; more confident.
