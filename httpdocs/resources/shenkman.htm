A Center Dedicated to the Study of Ronald Reagan

THE MARK R. SHENKMAN REAGAN RESEARCH CENTER

A one-of-a-kind Reagan resource, the Mark R. Shenkman Reagan Research Center strives to acquire and house every book written about Ronald W. Reagan. The center is open to the Eureka College community and scholars from around the world. The dedication was an landmark culmination to the Reagan Centennial Celebration at Eureka College (2011-2012) - a year that marked the 100th anniversary of Reagan’s birth.


The center is housed in the archives at Melick Library, which was itself dedicated by then-Gov. Ronald Reagan in 1967. The college is in the process of acquiring the estimated 1,000 books written about Reagan to add to its existing collection of primary documents, letters and objects in the archives and the Reagan museum.
“The announcement of the Mark R. Shenkman Reagan Research Center at Eureka College was an ideal way to celebrate the Ronald Reagan centennial,” said Eureka College President J. David Arnold.
“Mr. Shenkman’s gift will have a profound impact on all who value Reagan’s remarkable story of leadership and service.”

 

Eureka College received a major gift from Mark. R. Shenkman to create a Reagan research center in Melick Library on campus. The gift ranks among the most important in the history of the college’s relationship with the life and legacy of Ronald Reagan, who graduated from the college in 1932.


Shenkman is a member of the Ronald W. Reagan Society at Eureka College. He is the founder and president of Shenkman Capital Management Inc., an investment advisory firm with offices in New York City, London and Stamford, Conn. A resident of Greenwich, Conn., Shenkman received a bachelor’s degree in political science from the University of Connecticut and a master’s degree in business administration from The George Washington University,  Washington, D.C.
